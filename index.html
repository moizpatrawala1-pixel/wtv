💡 Things most tourists don't know
💡
Insider tips that make a difference
Save money, avoid mistakes, feel local
⌄
💵
Cash is king
Withdraw from ATMs at mall entrances — lower fees than standalone ATMs. Markets and street food are cash only. Carry ฿1,000–2,000 per person per day.
📱
Grab app
Set up before you land. Add a payment method in India — much easier than in Thailand. The only reliable way to get around Bangkok without stress.
🌧
July rain strategy
Rain comes in sharp afternoon bursts, not all day. Morning activities first, shelter mid-afternoon at pool or mall. Small waterproof bag is worth packing.
🛍
Bargaining rule
Start at 50% of asking price. Buy multiple items from one stall. Never buy from the first stall — compare 3 before committing to anything.
🍽
Ordering safely
"Mai sai moo" = no pork in Thai. Learn it — you'll use it at least 5 times. Always ask for chicken or beef versions when in doubt.
👗
Temple dress code
Phraya Nakhon Cave is sacred — shoulders and knees covered. Sarongs available at entrance for ฿20–30. Don't get caught out after hiking 40 minutes up.
🌡
Beat the heat
July is hot and humid. Coconut water from street vendors (~฿60) is better than bottled water and is absolutely everywhere. Don't underestimate the sun.
🛺
Tuktuk in Hua Hin
฿40–60 for short trips is fair. Agree price before getting in — always. In Bangkok, Grab wins on price and convenience every single time.
