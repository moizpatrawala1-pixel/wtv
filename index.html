D3
Phraya Nakhon Cave + Tamarind Market
Thu 3 Jul · The unmissable day trip
⌄
☀️ The sunbeam inside Phraya Nakhon Cave hits the royal pavilion between 10–11:30am. Your 7:30am departure is timed to arrive at exactly 10am. Don't sleep in.
7am
Light breakfast at hotel
Eat before you go — there's no good food until after the hike. Book the private car the evening before via Radisson concierge.
7:30am
Depart for Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park
~1 hour drive. Private car only — parking and navigation at the park are difficult and not worth the stress.
฿1,500–2,000 return (÷4 = ~฿450/person)
8:30am
Ban Bang Pu — park entry + longtail boat
Buy foreigner entry tickets at the gate. All 4 take the longtail boat to Laem Sala Beach (~10–15 min). The cave is not reachable by car — the boat is the only way in.
฿300/adult · ฿100/person each way (boat)
9am
Hike to Phraya Nakhon Cave
30–45 min uphill jungle trail. Moderately steep with steps cut into the rock. Closed shoes, 1.5L water each, watch your footing on the rocks. Take it slowly.
10am
Phraya Nakhon Cave + Kuha Karuhas Pavilion ⭐
The sunbeam hits the royal pavilion right now. Two connected chambers, a jungle canopy opening above, and a 19th-century Thai pavilion bathed in natural light. Stay 45–60 minutes. This will genuinely be the most beautiful thing you see on the trip.
11:30am
Hike down + boat back to car
Going down takes about 25 minutes. Boat back to the carpark.
12:30pm
Lunch — Kin Keng Restaurant
Ask the driver to stop here on the way back. Famous for crab and big portions. Order the stir-fried crab and tom yum — very good value.
฿200–300/person
2:30pm
Hotel — pool and proper rest
2.5 hours to recover from the hike. Use this time to pack for Bangkok tomorrow — everything ready tonight so the morning is clean.
5pm
Tamarind Market
Tuktuk 10 minutes from hotel. Local crowd, shaded by tamarind trees, zero tourist pressure. Graze across stalls for dinner: tom yum, grilled chicken, mango sticky rice. Your last Hua Hin evening — take it easy, wander slowly.
฿200–300/person
8pm
Hotel — final pack, early night
8am checkout tomorrow. Everything packed tonight.

Sat 5 Jul · Markets, racing, and the best halal dinner of the trip
⌄
7:30am
Breakfast at hotel
Eat properly. Chatuchak is a full marathon in the heat — you need fuel. Bring a big bag and wear comfortable shoes.

BTS Mo Chit → Chatuchak Market
Arrive at 9am — the earlier the better before the heat builds. Pick up the section map at the entrance. Sections 22–26 for home decor and gifts. Sections 5–6 for clothing. Sections 2–4 for jewellery. Find Saman Islam stall for lunch — ask locals inside for directions.

฿80–150/person

Easy Kart — all 4 🏎
Grab to Easy Kart (~฿100). Indoor circuit, full safety gear. Book 2 sessions each — the first run is a warmup, the second is where it gets competitive. One of those activities that's genuinely fun at any age.
฿350–500/session per person

Two hours to shower, rest, and get ready. Don't skip this — Usman is worth dressing for.

Michelin Bib Gourmand halal Thai on Sukhumvit Soi 22. Must-order: massaman curry, deep-fried seabass, tom yum, mango sticky rice. Order generously — it's all exceptional. Book ahead, fills up Saturday nights. Fully halal.

Iconsiam + Rajadamnern Muay Thai
Sun 6 Jul · Culture, shopping, and fight night
⌄
9:30am
Late breakfast at hotel
Earned rest after a big Day 5. No alarms, no rush. A slow morning before a brilliant evening.
11:30am
Grab to Iconsiam
฿80–120 by Grab, or take the BTS and Chao Phraya ferry for a more scenic ride. One of the most stunning malls in Southeast Asia — full riverfront views from inside.
12pm
Lunch — Sooksiam Food Court (ground floor)
Indoor floating market concept — regional Thai food from all 77 provinces. Chicken and seafood options clearly marked throughout. Mango sticky rice, tom kha, southern curries. The atmosphere alone is worth the trip.
฿200–350/person
1:30pm
Iconsiam — explore and shop
Sooksiam ground floor for quality Thai crafts, silk, and gifts. Upper floors for Apple, Uniqlo, and local brands. The riverside terrace is worth finding — great views of the Chao Phraya. Take it easy, 2 hours here feels right.
4pm
Hotel — rest and change
Grab back. 90 minutes to freshen up and get ready for Rajadamnern. Change into something comfortable.
5:30pm
Grab to Rajadamnern Stadium
~฿100. Arrive early — doors open 1 hour before the fights. Getting into your seats early is part of the experience: watch the trainers, the warm-ups, soak in the atmosphere before the first bell.
6pm
Rajadamnern Muay Thai — 8 bouts ⭐
The oldest and most prestigious Muay Thai venue in Thailand. Immersive dome projection inside, electric crowd, traditional betting atmosphere. The pre-fight wai kru ceremonies are mesmerising — each fighter performs a solo ritual before the first round. 3 hours of real Thai sport. Club Class seats are comfortable and perfectly positioned. One of the great live sporting experiences in Asia.
Club Class ฿1,900/person · Ringside ฿2,500/person
9:15pm
Late dinner — Soi Arab, Nana
Grab to Nana (~฿80). Soi 3/1 — Bangkok's Arab Street. Shawarma, kebabs, chicken fried rice, fresh mango juice. Open late, relaxed, no booking needed. Wind down after the fights.
฿150–250/person

Grab to Nusara — Old Bangkok
~฿150–200. Located at 336 Maha Rat Rd, near Wat Pho. The driver will know it. Arrive 15 minutes early — the rooftop view over Wat Pho at dusk is the moment you want to catch before being seated.

Dinner — Nusara ⭐⭐
Asia's #6 Best Restaurant 2025. Michelin-starred. Rooftop over Wat Pho at golden hour, olive-green interiors with gold accents, 12-course heritage Thai tasting menu. Thai Wagyu stir-fry with holy basil, seasonal fish in torch ginger salad, ancient curries from Rama V-era royal cookbooks, regional relishes, and desserts you won't find anywhere else. The menu naturally centres on chicken, beef, and seafood — mention your preference at booking. Allow 2.5 hours. This is the trip's final, transcendent meal. All four of you will remember it for years.
฿2,500–3,500/person (set menu)

Fly home ✈️
With the best memories.
