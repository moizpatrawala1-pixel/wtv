🛍 Shopping guide
🛍
Complete Bangkok Shopping Guide
Where to go, what to buy, floor-by-floor
⌄
💡 Golden rule: Always bargain. Start at 50–60% of asking price. Buying multiple items gets you lower per-unit prices everywhere. Bring cash — most stalls don't take cards. ATMs at every mall entrance.
MBK Center
8 floors, 2,000+ stalls. Designer bags, watches, sneakers (floors 1–3). Electronics floor 4. Food court floor 6 with halal options. Ask vendors for "back room" stock. Negotiate hard — 65% off asking is normal.
Dupes HQ Halal food
Platinum Fashion Mall
6 floors, 1,300+ shops. Wholesale model — more you buy, cheaper per item. Best for quality clothes, bags, shoes. AC and organised. 10-min walk from Ratchathewi BTS. Bring ฿3,000–5,000/person.
Quality fashion Wholesale
Chatuchak Market
Sat–Sun 9am–6pm. 15,000+ stalls, 27 sections. Sec 2–4: jewellery. Sec 5–6: clothing. Sec 22–26: home decor + gifts. Saman Islam stall: legendary halal khao mok gai.
Unique finds Sat 5 Jul ✅
Iconsiam — Sooksiam
Premium riverfront mall. Sooksiam (ground floor) = indoor floating market, quality regional Thai crafts and food from all 77 provinces. Great for authentic gifts, silk, ceramics. AC, clean, easy on the feet.
Upmarket Great for all
Siam Square + Centre
Local Thai streetwear — Greyhound, Carnival, Boyy. Siam Square outdoor alleys have younger local brands. Good for statement pieces that aren't generic tourist fare.
Local designer
Jodd Fairs (Night)
Night market on Ratchadapisek. Best for food + atmosphere rather than bulk buying. Halal food stalls throughout. Very photogenic. Go for the vibe.
Night vibes Halal ✅

Day 5 (Sat): Chatuchak 9am–2pm → Easy Kart → Usman dinner

D3
Phraya Nakhon Cave + Tamarind Market
Thu 3 Jul · Nature + last Hua Hin night
⌄
✅ Tamarind Market opens Thu–Sun from 5pm — you're good. Cicada is closed Thursdays, but Tamarind has more food variety anyway.
☀️ The sunbeam inside Phraya Nakhon Cave hits the royal pavilion at 10–11:30am. This is the entire point of going. Your 7am departure hits the cave at exactly 10am. Do not sleep in.
6:30am
Light breakfast at hotel
Eat light — there's a hike ahead. Book private car the evening before via hotel concierge.
7am
Depart for Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park
~1 hr drive. Private car only — parking and navigation is difficult. Not worth self-driving.
฿1,500–2,000 return car (split 4 = ~฿450/person)
8am
Ban Bang Pu — park tickets + longtail boat
Buy foreigner entry tickets. Longtail boat to Laem Sala Beach (10–15 min). All 4 fit in one boat. The cave is not reachable by car — the boat is mandatory.
฿300/adult entry · ฿100/person each way (boat)
8:30am
Hike up to Phraya Nakhon Cave
30–45 min uphill jungle trail. Moderately steep — take it at a comfortable pace, there are plenty of shade spots to rest. Wear closed shoes. Bring 1.5L water each. Steps are cut into the rock — watch your footing.
10am
Phraya Nakhon Cave + Kuha Karuhas Pavilion ⭐
The sunbeam hits the royal pavilion right now. Spend 45–60 min inside. Two connected chambers, completely surreal. One of the most memorable things you'll see on the entire trip. Photograph everything.
11:30am
Hike down + boat back
Easier going down — about 25 min. Boat back to the car.
12:30pm
Lunch — Kin Keng Halal Restaurant
Ask the driver to stop here on the way back. Famous halal crab and seafood. Big portions, great value. Order stir-fried crab and tom yum.
฿200–300/person
2pm
Hotel — afternoon rest + pool
3 hours to recover. Pool, nap, and start packing for Bangkok tomorrow. Bags ready tonight.
5pm
Tamarind Market (open Thu from 5pm)
Tuktuk 10 min from hotel. More food stalls than Cicada, very local crowd, shaded by tamarind trees. Graze for dinner — tom yum, grilled meats, mango sticky rice. Last Hua Hin night, keep it relaxed.
฿200–300/person for full dinner
8pm
Hotel — pack, early night
8am departure to Bangkok. Everything packed tonight so morning is smooth.
