Complete Bangkok Shopping Guide
Where to go, what to buy, floor-by-floor
⌄
💡 Golden rule: Always bargain. Start at 50–60% of asking price. Buying multiple items gets you lower per-unit prices. Bring cash — most stalls don't take cards. ATMs at every mall entrance.
MBK Center
8 floors, 2,000+ stalls. Designer bags, watches, sneakers (floors 1–3). Electronics floor 4. Halal food court floor 6. Ask for "back room" stock. Negotiate to 65% off asking.
Dupes HQ Halal food
Platinum Fashion Mall
6 floors, 1,300+ shops. Wholesale pricing — more you buy, cheaper per item. Best for quality clothes, bags, shoes. AC and organised. 10-min walk from Ratchathewi BTS.
Quality fashion Wholesale
Chatuchak Market
Sat–Sun only, 9am–6pm. 15,000+ stalls. Sec 22–26: home decor + gifts. Sec 5–6: clothing. Sec 2–4: jewellery. Saman Islam stall: legendary halal khao mok gai for lunch.
Unique finds Sat 5 Jul
Iconsiam — Sooksiam
Premium riverfront mall. Sooksiam (ground floor) = indoor floating market with quality crafts and food from all 77 Thai provinces. Great gifts. Easy on the feet.
Upmarket gifts Great for all
Jodd Fairs
Night market on Ratchadapisek. Best for food and atmosphere — some streetwear and accessories stalls too. Halal food throughout. Very photogenic.
Night vibes Halal ✅
Siam Square
Local Thai streetwear — Greyhound, Carnival, Boyy. Good for curated statement pieces. Combine with Jodd Fairs on Day 4 if you want an evening browse.

D2
Beach Day — Watersports + Sunset Dinner
Wed 2 Jul · The full Hua Hin experience
⌄
🌧 July is rainy season. If seas are rough, operators will cancel parasailing and banana boat — check conditions the evening before via hotel. Plan B: hotel spa + shopping in town. Don't pre-pay watersports online.
7am
Breakfast at hotel
Eat light — nothing heavy before time on the water. Radisson buffet if included, or fruit and bread.
9am
Walk to Hua Hin Beach — watersports
5–10 min from the Radisson. All vendors cluster near the main beach access. Negotiate as a group of 4 — package deals are standard and expected.
9:30am
Jet Ski
30-min sessions. Agree on the price before getting on — ฿600–800/30 min is the going rate. Everyone can rotate.
฿600–800/ski
10:30am
Parasailing
~10 min per person, tandem available. It's peaceful once you're up — a completely different perspective on the coastline. Check the rig before going up.
฿600–800/person
11:15am
Banana Boat — all 4 🎉
The funniest 15 minutes of the trip. Everyone falls off. That's exactly the point.
฿200–300/person
12pm
Hotel — shower, change, rest
A proper 1.5-hour break. The beach sun is intense — drink water and cool down before lunch.
1:30pm
Lunch — Wilaiwan Kitchen
Most popular halal Thai in Hua Hin. Go at 1:30pm to beat the 2pm rush. Famous pad Thai and massaman curry. Cash preferred, no reservations needed.
฿150–250/person
3pm
Long afternoon at Radisson pool
Three hours of nothing. Swim, nap, order a fresh juice from the pool bar, read. This is what the Radisson is for. No schedule, no rush.
6pm
Beach walk — sunset 🌅
Hua Hin is west-facing and the sunsets are genuinely spectacular. Walk down to the water, grab fresh coconuts from a vendor (฿60–80 each). Just sit, watch, and be somewhere together. One of the real moments of this trip.
7:30pm
Dinner — Sea Harmony Restaurant ⭐
Top-rated halal beachfront seafood. Fresh grilled fish, tom yum with fresh catch, prawn dishes. Book an outdoor table in advance and ask for beach-facing seating. The standout dinner of the Hua Hin leg — this is worth dressing up slightly for.
฿350–500/person
9:30pm
Back to hotel
7am start tomorrow. Don't stay out late.
D3
Phraya Nakhon Cave + Tamarind Market
Thu 3 Jul · The unmissable day trip
⌄
☀️ The sunbeam inside Phraya Nakhon Cave hits the royal pavilion between 10–11:30am. Your 7:30am departure is timed to arrive at exactly 10am. Don't sleep in.
7am
Light breakfast at hotel
Eat before you go — there's no good food until after the hike. Book the private car the evening before via Radisson concierge.
7:30am
Depart for Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park
~1 hour drive. Private car only — parking and navigation at the park are difficult and not worth the stress.
฿1,500–2,000 return (split 4 = ~฿450/person)
8:30am
Ban Bang Pu — park entry + longtail boat
Buy foreigner entry tickets at the gate. All 4 take the longtail boat to Laem Sala Beach (~10–15 min). The cave is not reachable by car — the boat is the only way in.
฿300/adult · ฿100/person each way (boat)
9am
Hike to Phraya Nakhon Cave
30–45 min uphill jungle trail. Moderately steep with steps cut into the rock — take it slowly, there are shade stops along the way. Closed shoes, 1.5L water each, watch your footing on the rocks.
10am
Phraya Nakhon Cave + Kuha Karuhas Pavilion ⭐
The sunbeam hits the royal pavilion right now. Two connected chambers, a jungle canopy opening above, and a 19th-century Thai pavilion bathed in natural light — it looks like something out of a film. Stay 45–60 minutes. Photograph everything. This will genuinely be the most beautiful thing you see on the trip.
11:30am
Hike down + boat back to car
Going down takes about 25 minutes. Boat back to the carpark.
12:30pm
Lunch — Kin Keng Halal Restaurant
Ask the driver to stop here on the way back. Famous for halal crab and big portions. Order the stir-fried crab and tom yum — very good value.
฿200–300/person
2:30pm
Hotel — pool and proper rest
2.5 hours to recover from the hike. Pool, nap, or just sit on the balcony. Use this time to pack for Bangkok tomorrow — everything ready tonight so the morning is clean.
5pm
Tamarind Market
Tuktuk 10 minutes from hotel. More variety than Cicada — local crowd, shaded by tamarind trees, zero tourist pressure. Graze across stalls for dinner: tom yum, grilled meats, mango sticky rice. This is your last Hua Hin evening. Take it easy, wander slowly.
฿200–300/person
8pm
Hotel — final pack, early night
8am checkout tomorrow. Everything packed tonight.

🚦 Friday Bangkok traffic adds 45+ minutes to the drive. Nothing planned before 2pm. Book the van the night before and leave Hua Hin at 8am sharp.

Checkout + van to Bangkok
3 hrs + traffic. Pre-booked private 7-seater. Load bags the night before if the hotel allows it.
฿2,500–3,000 total van ÷ 4
12pm
Check in — Holiday Inn Express Bangkok
Early check-in if available. Drop bags, freshen up, get changed.
1pm
Lunch — Yana Restaurant (MBK 5F)
Halal Thai and international, wide menu — great for the whole family after a long morning in a van. You're already in MBK; do a relaxed floor-by-floor browse after eating to get a feel for prices before you commit to anything.

MBK Center — browse and buy
Floors 1–3 for bags, accessories, cosmetics, sneakers, watches, and streetwear. Ask vendors to show "back room" stock for better quality. Start offers at 55–60% of asking price and compare across 3–4 stalls before buying anything. Take your time — there's no rush.

Jodd Fairs Night Market ⭐
Grab from hotel (~฿100). Bangkok's best night market right now — halal stalls throughout, grilled meats, seafood, Thai desserts. Photogenic and atmospheric. Wander, eat, and take it all in. Your first real Bangkok evening. Stay 2 hours.
฿250–400/person for dinner

D5
Chatuchak + Go-Karts + Usman
Sat 5 Jul · Markets, racing, and the best dinner of the trip
⌄
7:30am
Breakfast at hotel
Eat properly. Chatuchak is a full marathon in the heat — you need fuel. Bring a big bag and wear comfortable shoes.
9am
BTS Mo Chit → Chatuchak Market
Arrive at 9am — the earlier the better before the heat builds. Pick up the section map at the entrance. Sections 22–26 for home decor and gifts. Sections 5–6 for clothing. Sections 2–4 for jewellery and accessories. Find Saman Islam stall for lunch — ask locals inside for directions.
BTS ฿40–50/person
12pm
Lunch — Saman Islam (inside Chatuchak)
Legendary halal stall. The khao mok gai (Thai chicken biryani) is widely considered the best in Bangkok — don't leave the market without trying it. Arrive at noon before it sells out. Eat in the shade and rest your feet for 30 minutes.
฿80–150/person
1:30pm
Final sweep + exit by 2pm
It gets genuinely exhausting and hot after midday. Don't push it. Last pass for anything you missed, then leave. Cash only — budget ฿2,000–4,000/person if you're buying seriously.
2:30pm
Easy Kart — all 4 🏎
Grab to Easy Kart (~฿100). Indoor circuit, full safety gear. Book 2 sessions each in advance — the first run is always a warmup and the second is where it gets competitive. One of those activities that's genuinely fun at any age.
฿350–500/session per person
5pm
Hotel — rest and freshen up
Two hours to shower, rest, and get ready for the evening. Don't skip this — Usman is worth dressing for.
7pm
Dinner — Usman Thai Muslim Food ⭐
Michelin Bib Gourmand halal Thai on Sukhumvit Soi 22. This is one of the best meals of the trip. Must-order: massaman curry, deep-fried seabass, tom yum, mango sticky rice. Order generously — it's all exceptional. Book ahead, it fills up Saturday nights.
฿250–400/person
D6
Iconsiam + Rajadamnern Muay Thai
Sun 6 Jul · Culture, shopping, and fight night
⌄
9:30am
Late breakfast at hotel
Earned rest after a big Day 5. No alarms, no rush. A slow morning before a brilliant evening.
11:30am
Grab to Iconsiam
฿80–120 by Grab, or take the BTS and Chao Phraya ferry for a more scenic ride. One of the most stunning malls in Southeast Asia — full riverfront views from inside.
12pm
Lunch — Sooksiam Food Court (ground floor)
Indoor floating market concept — regional Thai food from all 77 provinces under one roof. Halal options clearly marked throughout. Mango sticky rice, khao mok, southern curries. The atmosphere alone is worth the trip here.
฿200–350/person
1:30pm
Iconsiam — explore and shop
Sooksiam ground floor for quality Thai crafts, silk, and gifts to take home. Upper floors for Apple, Uniqlo, and local brands. The riverside terrace is worth finding — great views of the Chao Phraya. Take it easy, 2 hours here feels right.
4pm
Hotel — rest and change
Grab back. 90 minutes to freshen up and get ready for Rajadamnern. Change into something comfortable — it's an arena, not a restaurant.
5:30pm
Grab to Rajadamnern Stadium
~฿100. Arrive early — doors open 1 hour before the fights. Getting into your seats with time to spare is part of the experience: watch the trainers, the warm-ups, soak in the atmosphere before the first bell.
6pm
Rajadamnern Muay Thai — 8 bouts ⭐
The oldest and most prestigious Muay Thai venue in Thailand. Immersive dome projection inside, electric crowd, traditional betting atmosphere that fills the whole arena. The pre-fight wai kru ceremonies are mesmerising — each fighter performs a solo ritual before the first round. 3 hours of real Thai sport. Club Class seats are comfortable and perfectly positioned. One of the great live sporting experiences in Asia — all 4 of you will remember this night.
Club Class ฿1,900/person · Ringside ฿2,500/person
9:15pm
Late dinner — Soi Arab, Nana
Grab to Nana (~฿80). Soi 3/1 — Bangkok's Arab Street. Shawarma, kebabs, halal fried rice, fresh mango juice. Open late, relaxed, no booking needed. Wind down after the fights.
฿150–250/person
D7
Muay Thai + Massages + The Local + Home
Mon 7 Jul · Last day — 11:45pm flight
⌄
7:30am
Breakfast at hotel — all 4
Eat well. Long day ahead.
9am
Moiz + Idris → Yokkao Training Centre
Book beginner drop-in at yokkao.com before the trip. 2-hr session — combinations, pad work, light sparring. A proper Muay Thai gym, not a tourist show. Bring hand wraps (available at the gym). Shoes off, respect the space.
฿600–1,000/person
9am
Parents — slow morning at the hotel
Second breakfast, pool, balcony time. A proper holiday morning with no agenda before the group reunites.
10am
All 4 — massages at Asia Herb Association ⭐
Book 4 slots in advance at asiaherbassociation.com. 1.5–2 hr traditional Thai massage — the real thing, not a tourist spa. Consistently rated the best value in Bangkok. The perfect way to start the last day together. You'll walk out feeling completely different.
฿350–450/hr · per person
12pm
Freshen up + get changed
Short break at the hotel. Change into something nice — The Local deserves it.
1pm
Lunch — The Local by Oamthong ⭐
Heritage Thai fine dining. Tablecloths, beautiful presentation, recipes that have been in Thai families for generations. Order from the chicken, beef, and seafood dishes — mention no pork when booking. Must-order: miang kham (leaf wraps), massaman, steamed sea bass in lime broth, coconut pudding dessert. This is the trip's sendoff — take your time here, order generously, and enjoy it. Book 2 weeks in advance.
฿500–700/person
3:30pm
Final shopping — MBK or Platinum
MBK if you missed anything on Day 4. Platinum for last-minute clothing and bulk buys. Hard exit at 5pm — you need to be back to pack.
5pm
Hotel — pack everything
2 hours. Weigh bags against your airline limit. Photograph everyone's passports as a backup. Check in online if your airline allows it. Group all your receipts.
7pm
Light dinner — hotel or nearby
Nothing complicated. Hotel restaurant or a halal spot within walking distance. Save your energy for the airport.
฿150–200/person
8:30pm
Pre-booked Grab to Suvarnabhumi
Book via Grab app the morning of (฿250–350). Do not take BTS + Airport Rail Link with luggage — it's not worth the chaos.
11:45pm
Fly home ✈️
With the best memories.
