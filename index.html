💡 Things most tourists don't know
💡
Insider tips that make a difference
Save money · avoid mistakes · feel local
⌄
💵
Cash is king
Withdraw from mall ATMs — lower fees. Markets and street food are cash only. Carry ฿1,000–2,000 per person per day in Bangkok.
📱
Grab app
Set up before you land. Add a payment method in India — far easier than in Thailand. The only reliable way around Bangkok.
🌧
July rain
Sharp afternoon bursts, not all day. Morning activities first, shelter mid-afternoon at pool or mall. Small waterproof bag is worth packing.
🛍
Bargaining rule
Start at 50% of asking price. Buy multiple from one stall. Never buy from the first stall — compare 3 before committing to anything.
🍽
Ordering safely
"Mai sai moo" = no pork in Thai. Learn it — you'll use it at least 5 times a day. Always ask for chicken or beef versions when uncertain.
👗
Temple dress
Phraya Nakhon Cave is sacred — shoulders and knees covered. Sarongs at entrance for ฿20–30. Don't get caught out after hiking 40 minutes up.
🌡
Beat the heat
Coconut water from street vendors (~฿60) beats bottled water every time. July is hot and humid — don't underestimate the midday sun.
🛺
Tuktuk in Hua Hin
฿40–60 for short trips is fair. Agree the price before getting in — always. In Bangkok, Grab wins on price and convenience every time.
